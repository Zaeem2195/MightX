# Sales-Tech Outreach Angles

Use this file as the messaging companion to the GTM Engine while validating the sales-tech wedge.

## Positioning sentence

> We run a weekly competitive intelligence briefing for sales-tech revenue teams so reps do not walk into calls blind when a competitor changes pricing, launches a feature, or starts getting hammered in reviews.

## Primary email angle

Subject ideas:

- `{{CompanyName}} vs {{TopCompetitor}}`
- `keeping reps unblindsided`
- `weekly competitor briefings`

Core body angle (must match `gtm-engine/prompts/personalization.txt` — **no** generic “sample for your space”):

- Name **1–2 real competitors** they face.
- Close with a **custom weekend baseline** on those names and offer Monday findings, e.g.  
  `I'm setting up my intelligence engine this week. If I configure a baseline capture on [Competitor A] and [Competitor B], would you be opposed to me sending over the findings next Monday?`

## Why this angle works

It avoids abstract "AI service" framing and leads directly with:

- competitor pressure
- rep preparedness
- concrete monitored signals
- a low-friction ask that you can actually fulfill (custom run, not a pre-built category PDF)

## Discovery call framing

Use these questions:

1. How does your team find out when a competitor changes something important?
2. Have reps been caught off-guard by a competitor on live calls this quarter?
3. Does anyone actually own competitor tracking, or is it scattered?
4. If your team got a useful Monday briefing every week, who would use it first?

## Pilot offer

Recommended first offer:

- 3–4 competitors
- weekly briefing
- 2–4 week pilot
- manual QA on every report
- discounted paid pilot preferred

## When to expand the offer

Only after a prospect reacts well to the **custom** report you sent should you talk about:

- dashboards
- trigger alerts
- deeper enablement use cases
- quarterly summaries

Sell the report first. Expand later.
