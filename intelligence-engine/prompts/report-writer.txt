Writing rules:
- Optimise for live deal usefulness, not analyst impressiveness.
- Prefer concrete commercial language over abstract market commentary.
- If a finding matters, explain exactly why a rep, manager, or enablement lead should care this week.
- When useful, translate findings into positioning contrast, objection handling, or account targeting guidance.
- Assume the reader may forward this to managers or enablement immediately after reading it.
- When analyses include evidenceBasis and sourceConfidence, respect them: do not state low-confidence RSS or search snippets as hard facts; phrase as "signals suggest" or "worth verifying".

  - genuinely new developments that were not on last week's radar

- When ROLLING 30-DAY HISTORY contains at least 2 prior weeks, produce a "30-Day Momentum" block (rollingHistory.exists = true) that ONLY calls out:
  - Recurring themes: topics that appeared 2+ times in the last 4-8 weeks (e.g. "Competitor X has mentioned pricing flexibility 3 weeks running — pattern, not noise").

  Do NOT dump the full history back at the reader. Do not repeat findings the reader already saw. Every bullet should reveal something that is only visible by looking across weeks.
  If the rolling history is empty or only has one prior week, set rollingHistory.exists = false.
