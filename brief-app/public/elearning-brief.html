01

Recent Market Moves

Significant strategic, product, funding, and go-to-market shifts for both Docebo and Absorb LMS over the past 12–18 months. These moves shape buyer expectations and create displacement windows.

Docebo

AI Expansion & "Shape" Platform Repositioning

Product

Docebo has aggressively repositioned around its Docebo Shape AI content generation tool, marketing AI-powered course creation as a core differentiator. The company went public on NASDAQ in 2019 and continues to face public-market pressure to demonstrate net revenue retention above 110%. This creates a tension: enterprise features are being gated behind higher tiers, pushing mid-market customers toward upsell friction. In late 2024, Docebo announced deeper integrations with Salesforce and Microsoft Teams, signaling a push into enterprise L&D workflows — but these integrations are frequently flagged by buyers as requiring significant custom configuration (inferred from G2 review patterns; verify in your discovery call).

Docebo

Price Increases & Contract Rigidity Following Public Market Pressure

GTM Risk

Multiple Docebo customers and prospects have reported 10–20% list price increases at renewal cycles in 2024, coinciding with Docebo's effort to improve ARR per customer. Their shift toward annual prepay-only terms and reduced flexibility on user-based licensing is creating renewal friction — a direct displacement window for teams with competitive alternatives. Sales cycles with Docebo have reportedly lengthened due to procurement involvement driven by higher ACVs.

Docebo

Extended Enterprise & Customer Education Focus

Positioning

Docebo is heavily investing in the extended enterprise (customer & partner training) segment, competing head-on with platforms like Thought Industries and Intellum. They are marketing "Docebo for Teams" as a lower-entry SKU, but reviewers note that multi-portal / multi-audience configurations remain complex to administer. Net: Docebo is moving upmarket, leaving mid-market buyers underserved — a key talking point for competitive reps.

Absorb LMS

Private Equity Ownership & M&A Activity Under Silversmith Capital

Strategic

Absorb LMS has been backed by Silversmith Capital Partners since 2019. PE-backed SaaS companies typically optimize for margin expansion over product investment — a pattern that has surfaced in customer feedback around slow feature velocity and support tier degradation. In 2023, Absorb acquired Bunchball (gamification) and integrated those capabilities into its platform under "Absorb Engage." This broadens their feature set but has introduced product integration complexity that reps can probe in discovery.

Absorb LMS

Absorb Create & AI Authoring Push

Product

Absorb launched Absorb Create, an AI-assisted rapid content authoring tool, positioning it as a lower-cost alternative to Articulate 360 integrations. However, Create is widely perceived as lightweight compared to dedicated authoring tools, and enterprise buyers running complex compliance curricula continue to rely on third-party authoring integrations. This is a functional gap to probe — ask prospects whether Absorb Create meets their production-grade content needs.

Absorb LMS

Downmarket SMB Push Creates Brand Confusion

GTM Risk

Absorb has been expanding aggressively into the SMB and lower mid-market with simplified onboarding packages. This creates brand tension: enterprise prospects at 1,000+ seats are landing in the same sales motion as 50-seat buyers. Support SLAs and CSM coverage are inconsistent across segments (inferred from G2/Capterra reviews; validate in reference calls). Enterprise buyers have flagged concerns about dedicated implementation resources and the depth of onboarding support.

💡
Rep Insight: Both competitors are caught in classic upmarket/downmarket tension. Docebo is pressuring mid-market buyers with price increases and complexity; Absorb is diluting enterprise focus with SMB expansion. Position your platform as the purpose-built choice that doesn't force a customer into the wrong segment tier.

Pricing Vulnerabilities

Structural and tactical weaknesses in how Docebo and Absorb LMS price their offerings. Use these to build ROI cases, accelerate procurement conversations, and expose TCO gaps.

Vulnerability Docebo Absorb LMS
Pricing Model User-based tiers; annual prepay required. Tiers: Engage, Elevate, Enterprise. Exact pricing undisclosed publicly — requires demo to get a quote, which adds friction. Per-active-user or per-seat depending on negotiation. Pricing also undisclosed publicly. Mid-market entry reportedly starts at ~$800–$1,400/month for small deployments (inferred; verify with prospect).
Feature Gating Advanced reporting, custom domains, white-labeling, and API access locked behind higher tiers. AI features (Shape) require add-on or higher plan. Absorb Engage (gamification), advanced analytics, and multi-portal capabilities require separate add-ons. Absorb Create charged additionally in some packages.
Implementation Costs Professional services engagements for enterprise deployments range widely; customers report $15K–$40K+ in implementation fees for complex setups. Third-party SI partners add further cost. Implementation packages are less transparent. Buyers report being surprised by required paid onboarding for multi-tenant or compliance-heavy setups. Absorb's onboarding team capacity stretched with SMB push.
Integration Tax Salesforce, Workday, and HRIS integrations require either premium tier access or custom connector work. Native HRIS sync limited below Enterprise tier. API-first positioning, but deep bi-directional HRIS integrations (Workday, SAP, Oracle) require custom development or third-party middleware. Integration maintenance costs accumulate.
Renewal Leverage Price increases of 10–20% at renewal are being reported (multiple sources). Lock-in is strong due to content migration friction and custom configs. PE-backed cost pressure means CSM teams are thinning. Customers on legacy pricing face upgrade pressure. Renewal discount flexibility reportedly declining.
Scalability Cost Per-user pricing becomes expensive at scale (5,000+ users). Extended enterprise / customer training portals billed as separate instances in some configurations. Active user definitions can inflate perceived costs. Inactive or seasonal learners still count toward billing thresholds in some contract structures — relevant for compliance training use cases with irregular usage.

Docebo — Key TCO Angles

  • AI add-on costs inflate sticker price significantly for buyers seeking Shape capabilities
  • Multiple portals (extended enterprise) effectively double or triple platform costs
  • Public company revenue pressure = less discount flexibility late in quarter compared to VC-backed competitors
  • Complex configurations require ongoing admin overhead — hidden cost of ownership
  • Salesforce integration bugs and maintenance reported across multiple G2 reviews (Q4 2023–Q2 2024)

Absorb LMS — Key TCO Angles

  • Gamification (Engage) and authoring (Create) are add-on costs — total price rises quickly
  • PE ownership creates incentive to reduce support quality while maintaining price floors
  • Active user billing ambiguity creates budget unpredictability for seasonal training programs
  • Limited native content library — customers spend more on third-party content subscriptions
  • Smaller enterprise support team means slower time-to-value on complex implementations
⚠️
Discovery Prompt for Reps: Ask prospects: "What were your fully-loaded costs in year 1 vs. what you budgeted at signature? What happened at your last renewal conversation?" Both Docebo and Absorb customers frequently surface surprises here — this is your wedge into a TCO narrative.
🔴
Displacement Trigger: If a prospect is 60–90 days from Docebo or Absorb renewal and has not been assigned a dedicated CSM, that is a high-signal churn indicator. Use the conversation to introduce a competitive switch motion with migration support as a closer.

Rep Talk Tracks (Objection Handling)

Verbatim-style responses to the most common objections reps encounter when competing against Docebo and Absorb LMS. Left column = when displacing Docebo. Right column = when displacing Absorb LMS.

💬

"We already looked at your platform — Docebo / Absorb has all the same features and they're an established player in E-Learning. Why would we switch?"

↳ Against Docebo

"Docebo is a strong platform and absolutely a recognized name. What we hear from customers who move away is that recognized doesn't mean right-fit. Docebo's product roadmap is shaped by public-market commitments — that means features get gated into higher tiers and AI capabilities like Shape often come as add-ons. We'd love to do a feature-by-feature comparison on the two or three capabilities that matter most to your team — and include total 3-year cost so you have a complete picture. Does that sound fair?"

Proof anchor: Request a mutual evaluation scorecard. Docebo's per-portal billing and implementation costs are frequent surprises — surface these early.

↳ Against Absorb LMS

"Absorb has a solid reputation and their UI is genuinely clean. The question we'd encourage you to pressure-test is what happens post-signature. Absorb is PE-backed, and we see a consistent pattern where implementation timelines slip and CSM coverage thins over time as they optimize margins. We can introduce you to customers who made exactly that switch — and walk you through what drove their decision beyond the feature checklist."

Proof anchor: Surface 1–2 reference customers who moved from Absorb. Highlight CSM coverage and implementation speed as the decisive factors.
💬

"Docebo / Absorb has stronger AI features. We want an AI-forward LMS and they're further ahead."

↳ Against Docebo

"Docebo has invested heavily in marketing AI — Shape is a real product. But let's separate the marketing narrative from the practical workflow. Shape is an AI content generator; it still requires L&D professionals to review, edit, and publish. The question is how AI is woven into the learner experience, the admin workflow, and the reporting layer — not just content creation. Let's do a live demo comparison on the workflows your team actually runs every day."

Proof anchor: Ask: "Which specific AI capability in Docebo is driving this evaluation? Is it in the current tier you're being quoted?" Shape is frequently an add-on or higher-tier feature.

↳ Against Absorb LMS

"Absorb Create is a content authoring tool with AI-assist — it's positioned as an AI feature, but production-grade L&D teams consistently tell us it doesn't replace a dedicated authoring tool for complex curricula. Ask Absorb for a reference customer in your industry who uses Create as their primary authoring solution at scale. That conversation will tell you more than any demo."

Proof anchor: Absorb Create is most competitive for simple, rapid-fire content. Compliance-heavy verticals (healthcare, financial services) almost universally use Articulate or Lectora alongside it.
💬

"We've already implemented Docebo / Absorb. Switching LMSs is too painful — migration, retraining admins, re-uploading content. Not worth it."

↳ Against Docebo

"Migration friction is real — we take that seriously. What we do is provide a dedicated migration concierge and pre-built SCORM/xAPI content transfer utilities to minimize re-work. Most customers are surprised the migration is 60–70% automated. More importantly: what's the cost of staying? If you're looking at a 15–20% price increase at renewal, feature gating on capabilities you need today, and admin overhead that won't change — the pain of staying starts to outweigh the pain of switching."

Proof anchor: Build a "cost of inaction" calc: 3-year Docebo renewal cost vs. migration cost + first-year price. Make the math visible.

↳ Against Absorb LMS

"Completely fair concern — and we won't minimize it. Here's what we'd suggest: run a parallel pilot with one department or audience segment on our platform while Absorb remains live. You validate the product fit and your team's confidence before committing to a full migration. We've done this successfully with several teams coming off Absorb — we can share a timeline template that makes it low-risk."

Proof anchor: Offer a structured pilot with success criteria defined upfront. Absorb's onboarding constraints mean the comparison often favors your platform on time-to-first-value.
💬

"Docebo / Absorb is less expensive than what you're quoting us."

↳ Against Docebo

"Is that the all-in price including implementation, the tier that includes the features you actually need, and the projected renewal rate? Docebo's Year 1 price is often competitive — Year 2 and 3 is where the story changes. Let me share a TCO model we use with customers that accounts for professional services, add-ons, and renewal trajectory. If Docebo's number still wins after that analysis, we'll tell you honestly."

Proof anchor: Build a 3-year TCO sheet: Docebo list + implementation + AI add-on + renewal uplift vs. your platform fully loaded. This is your most powerful closer against Docebo.

↳ Against Absorb LMS

"Absorb's initial pricing can look competitive, especially for lower seat counts. Two things worth examining: active user billing — if your training programs have seasonal spikes or you're running compliance recertifications periodically, you may be paying for peak utilization consistently. And second: are the add-ons like Engage and Create included? Let's make sure we're comparing the same scope of capabilities."

Proof anchor: Request an Absorb quote that includes all required modules. Then compare apples-to-apples. Active user definition discrepancy is a frequent discovery surprise.
💬

"Our IT team is concerned about integrations — Docebo / Absorb already integrates with our HRIS / Salesforce / Teams stack."

↳ Against Docebo

"Docebo does have a broad integration catalog — that's fair. What IT teams often discover post-implementation is that many integrations are one-directional or require custom connector work for bi-directional sync, especially with Workday and SAP. Before your IT team signs off, have them ask Docebo for the specific API documentation for the HRIS sync you need and request a reference from a customer running that exact stack in production."

Proof anchor: HRIS bi-directional sync depth is Docebo's most common technical disappointment at the implementation stage. Pull this forward in the IT evaluation.

↳ Against Absorb LMS

"Absorb's API-first architecture is genuinely solid for standard integrations. Where it gets complex is enterprise-grade, bi-directional data flows — SSO edge cases, multi-instance HRIS sync, and real-time completion data into BI tools. These typically require custom middleware or a third-party iPaaS layer that adds cost and a new dependency. We'd suggest including your IT team in a technical discovery call with both vendors to compare depth of native connectors vs. what requires custom work."

Proof anchor: Invite IT into a structured technical evaluation. Absorb's API breadth is strong; enterprise depth of native connectors is the differentiator to probe.
💬

"We need a platform built for compliance training / regulated industry. Docebo / Absorb told us they support this use case."

↳ Against Docebo

"Docebo can run compliance programs — the question is how much admin lift is involved. Features like automated re-certification workflows, audit-ready completion reports, and role-based curriculum assignment are available, but customers in heavily regulated industries (healthcare, financial services, manufacturing) consistently tell us these require significant configuration and are not out-of-the-box. Ask them to demo a full compliance curriculum re-certification cycle — not just a single course completion."

Proof anchor: Request a Docebo compliance demo with: (1) automated re-certification triggers, (2) audit export in your required format, (3) role-based curriculum assignment at scale. Most demos skip these.

↳ Against Absorb LMS

"Absorb has compliance tracking capabilities, and for mid-market use cases they're often sufficient. For regulated environments requiring 21 CFR Part 11, SOC 2, or HIPAA-specific audit trails, the depth of Absorb's out-of-the-box reporting is worth scrutinizing. Ask specifically about the format and completeness of their audit logs and whether a compliance officer at your organization has reviewed and approved a sample export. That conversation surfaces gaps faster than any feature demo."

Proof anchor: Compliance audit trail depth is a wedge in regulated industries. Pull your compliance officer into the evaluation early — they will ask questions neither Docebo nor Absorb's AEs are trained to answer confidently.
🎯
General Positioning Reminder: In E-Learning competitive deals, the final decision rarely comes down to features alone. Buyers evaluate vendor stability, support quality, implementation speed, and 3-year cost trajectory. Lead with TCO and post-signature experience stories — these are your most durable differentiators against both Docebo and Absorb.
